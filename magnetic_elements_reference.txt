MOST MAGNETIC ELEMENTS - REFERENCE LIST
========================================

This reference list provides typical magnetic moments per atom (in Bohr magnetons, μB)
for the most magnetic elements. Values are approximate and can vary depending on
crystal structure, temperature, and magnetic ordering.

NOTES:
------
1. Magnetic moments are given in Bohr magnetons (μB)
2. Values are for elemental forms at low temperatures
3. In compounds, moments can vary significantly
4. Rare earth elements have the highest moments due to unpaired 4f electrons
5. 3d transition metals (Fe, Co, Ni) are most commonly used in applications
6. Magnetic ordering (ferromagnetic, antiferromagnetic, etc.) affects net moment
